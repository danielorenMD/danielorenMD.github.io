About

I'm Daniel Oren, a physician-scientist in cardiology. I serve as an Instructor in Medicine in the Division of Cardiology at Columbia University Irving Medical Center and as a Clinical Research Scientist at Columbia's Center for Advanced Cardiac Care.

My work sits at the intersection of clinical cardiology and translational research in advanced heart failure and heart transplantation. A central thread is the use of molecular tools — donor-derived cell-free DNA (dd-cfDNA), gene expression profiling, and the molecular microscope (MMDx) — to detect allograft rejection earlier and more precisely than histology alone. I'm also interested in mechanical circulatory support, inflammatory myocardial and pericardial syndromes, and cardio-oncology, including the care of cancer survivors undergoing transplantation.

I've contributed to studies published in journals including Circulation: Heart Failure, the Journal of the American Heart Association, the Journal of Heart and Lung Transplantation, the Journal of Cardiac Failure, and JACC: CardioOncology. A full list is available on my Google Scholar profile.

This site is where I share that research and write about cardiovascular science for a broader audience. Posts reflect my own views and are intended for general interest and education — nothing here is medical advice.

You can find me on Google Scholar and LinkedIn, or reach me by email.
