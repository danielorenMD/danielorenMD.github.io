Daniel Oren, MD, MSc

Instructor in Medicine · Division of Cardiology, Columbia University Irving Medical Center

Daniel Oren is a physician-scientist in cardiology at Columbia University Irving Medical Center, where his clinical and research work centers on advanced heart failure and heart transplantation. He is a board-certified internal medicine physician and an American Heart Association-certified ACLS, BLS, and PALS instructor. A central thread of his research is the use of molecular tools such as donor-derived cell-free DNA (dd-cfDNA), gene expression profiling, and the molecular microscope (MMDx) to detect allograft rejection earlier and more precisely than histology alone. His work also spans mechanical circulatory support, inflammatory cardiomyopathies, and cardio-oncology.

On this site, Daniel writes about medical science, with a focus on cardiovascular medicine, internal medicine, and resuscitation science, sharing research, methods, and perspectives drawn from clinical practice.
