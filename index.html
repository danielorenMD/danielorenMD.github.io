Daniel Oren, MD, MSc

Instructor in Medicine · Division of Cardiology, Columbia University Irving Medical Center

I'm a physician-scientist in cardiology at Columbia University Irving Medical Center, where my clinical and research work centers on advanced heart failure and heart transplantation. A central thread is the use of molecular tools — donor-derived cell-free DNA (dd-cfDNA), gene expression profiling, and the molecular microscope (MMDx) — to detect allograft rejection earlier and more precisely than histology alone. I'm also engaged in research on mechanical circulatory support, inflammatory cardiomyopathies, and cardio-oncology. This site is where I share that research and write about cardiovascular science.
